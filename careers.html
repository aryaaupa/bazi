CAREERS AT BAZI

Build systems that know when to change.

Bazi is building adaptive AI infrastructure for software-based medicine. We are looking for people who can work across technical rigor, clinical responsibility and human complexity without reducing any of them to a slogan.

Explore Open Roles Introduce Yourself

THE WORK

Not another engagement dashboard.

The problem is not simply predicting disengagement. The harder work is deciding what may change, what must remain fixed and how a system should behave when evidence is incomplete.

01 · TECHNICAL DEPTH

Build the decision layer.

Work on adaptive inference, event-driven systems, feedback loops, model evaluation and the infrastructure connecting therapeutic software to bounded actions.

02 · CLINICAL RESTRAINT

Design limits deliberately.

Bazi recommends adaptations within provider-approved boundaries. Authority, uncertainty and escalation paths are product architecture, not fine print.

03 · HUMAN COMPLEXITY

Respect what signals cannot explain.

A skipped session is evidence, not a diagnosis. We want people who can build useful systems without pretending that observable behavior is the whole person.

We are not hiring people to make healthcare software feel more intelligent.

We are hiring people to make intelligence more responsible.

The strongest candidates will be comfortable saying “I do not know yet” and disciplined enough to convert that uncertainty into an experiment, a design constraint or a better question.

You do not need to arrive with every domain already mastered. You do need unusually high standards, intellectual honesty and the ability to learn across boundaries.

01

You think in systems.

You notice dependencies, failure modes and second-order effects rather than optimizing a local metric in isolation.

02

You write clearly.

Technical decisions, model behavior and product boundaries should be explainable without hiding behind abstraction.

03

You challenge assumptions.

You are willing to question the prompt, the metric and the architecture before polishing an answer to the wrong problem.

04

You care about consequences.

In healthcare, a technically elegant decision can still be irresponsible. You consider who bears the cost of error.

THE PROCESS

Clear, demanding and respectful of your time.

We want enough signal to make a serious decision without turning an interview process into unpaid consulting work.

01

Introduction

A focused conversation about your work, judgment and what you want to build next.

02

Working Session

A live problem-solving discussion using a realistic but self-contained Bazi scenario.

03

Deep Dive

Walk us through something you built, researched, designed or changed your mind about.

04

Mutual Decision

Direct conversations about expectations, ownership, working style and what success would actually require.

The right person may not match a role we have named yet.

Send a concise introduction, the problem at Bazi you would want to own and one example of work that shows how you think. A conventional résumé is useful, but it is not the entire application.
