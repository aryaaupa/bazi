THE ENGAGEMENT PROBLEM

Treatment software often reacts after the patient has already left.

Digital therapeutics can deliver structured treatment at scale, but many still follow a fixed path. A missed session may trigger a reminder, yet the system rarely understands the gradual behavioral pattern that led to disengagement.

Bazi analyzes changes across completion, timing, duration, fatigue and treatment difficulty so therapeutic products can respond before a temporary decline becomes abandonment.

FOR COMPANIES

Build a therapeutic product that responds before patients leave.

Bazi helps digital therapeutic companies, virtual care platforms and rehabilitation products detect developing disengagement using behavioral signals already generated by their software.

The platform then evaluates a constrained set of provider-approved actions and recommends the response most likely to restore meaningful engagement.

THE BUSINESS CASE

Disengagement is not only a patient-experience problem. It is a product-performance problem.

When patients stop participating, treatment completion declines, outcome measurement becomes harder and the value of the therapeutic product becomes more difficult to demonstrate.

Bazi gives product and clinical teams infrastructure for recognizing that risk earlier and evaluating an appropriate response.

HOW BAZI WORKS

A closed-loop intelligence layer for software-based care.

01

Observe behavior

Bazi receives product signals such as completion, session length, response timing, reported fatigue and difficulty feedback.

02

Estimate risk

The system identifies sustained behavioral change and estimates the probability of near-term disengagement.

03

Select an intervention

Bazi evaluates only actions approved by the therapeutic provider, such as pacing changes, reminder adjustments or clinician escalation.

04

Adapt and learn

The therapeutic product delivers the approved adaptation and measures whether engagement improves.

PATIENT PRINCIPLES

Adaptive care should remain understandable, bounded and human-accountable.

01

Visible adaptation

02

Appropriate human oversight

Significant treatment decisions should preserve human review and clear responsibility.

03

Defined clinical boundaries

Adaptations should remain within actions approved by the therapeutic organization or clinical team.

04

Responsible data use

Sensitive behavioral and health information should be handled with care, transparency and appropriate controls.

A patient should never be reduced to an engagement score.
