NOTES WRITTEN WHILE BUILDING BAZI

Marginalia.

Observations collected in the margins of books, papers, conversations and failed experiments.

These are not conclusions, instructions or product claims. They are fragments of a company still deciding what adaptive medicine should become.

Motion Engagement Judgment Intent Feedback Limits
001

PLATO, PRESERVING HERACLITUS

Cratylus

All things are in motion and nothing remains still.

A classical claim about reality, encountered here as a design problem.

BAZI MARGIN

Therapeutic software often behaves as though the patient who enrolled is the patient who remains.

Heraclitus would object.

The difficult part may not be personalization. It may be recognizing when yesterday’s personalization has already become obsolete.
?
002

STRAUSS ET AL.

Meaningful Engagement in Digital Therapeutics

Engagement is not simply the amount of product use.

A distinction between measurable activity and activity that contributes to therapeutic value.

BAZI MARGIN

Most products ask:

Did the patient complete the session?

We keep wondering whether the earlier question is:

Did the treatment still fit the person?

Engagement may be a consequence of fit, not the thing to optimize directly.

A WORKING DISTINCTION

Adaptation is not personalization.

Personalization selects an experience for a person. Adaptation asks whether that experience should remain the same after the person changes.

003

HERBERT SIMON

Bounded Rationality

Every decision is made inside limits.

Limited information. Limited time. Limited models. Limited certainty.

BAZI MARGIN

Prediction is cheap.

Judgment is expensive.

A risk score can say what may happen. It cannot silently decide what ought to happen next.

The harder system begins after prediction.
004

REGULATORY LANGUAGE

Intended Use

What is the software intended to do?

A regulatory question that is also a philosophical one.

BAZI MARGIN

Intent matters before accuracy.

A perfectly accurate system can still be built for the wrong purpose.

We should be able to say not only what Bazi predicts, but what authority it refuses to claim.

Limits are part of the product, not legal language added after the product exists.
see also: restraint
005

NORBERT WIENER

Cybernetics

A system changes by observing the consequences of action.

Feedback as the relationship between intervention, response and correction.

BAZI MARGIN

A recommendation is not adaptive merely because a model produced it.

The system becomes adaptive only when it notices whether the recommendation helped.

Otherwise it is automation wearing the language of learning.
006

MARCUS AURELIUS

Meditations

The universe is transformation. Life is opinion.

Observation changes. Interpretation changes. The distinction remains essential.

BAZI MARGIN

The signal is not the person.

A delayed session may mean fatigue, fear, travel, worsening symptoms, distraction or nothing at all.

Confidence should never be confused with comprehension.

Still thinking about how software can express that honestly.
007

DONALD NORMAN

Human-Centered Design

The problem is rarely only the interface.

The visible product is only one part of the system that shapes human action.

BAZI MARGIN

The interface is where adaptation appears.

The product is the judgment that decided whether it should.

We may be designing a decision boundary more than a screen.
008

UNFINISHED

A note not yet written

When should an adaptive system choose not to adapt?

NO MARGIN YET

We have not decided.

Some questions should remain visible before their answers become convenient.
?

We would rather show the question before pretending to own the answer.

Marginalia will change as Bazi changes. Notes may be revised, contradicted, removed or left unfinished. That is the point.
