The problem

Digital treatment works only when patients stay with it.

Digital therapeutics can support treatment across mental health, neurological care, chronic disease, pain management, and physical rehabilitation.

Depression and anxietyADHDChronic painStroke recoveryMusculoskeletal rehabilitationChronic disease management

Most digital therapeutics follow predefined treatment paths, but patients respond differently. Disengagement often develops gradually, and most systems react only after engagement has already been lost.

Lower adherence can reduce both clinical value and commercial performance.

Traditional digital therapeutic

  • Fixed treatment sequence
  • Same logic for broad patient groups
  • Responds after inactivity
  • Limited personalization

Bazi-enabled therapeutic

  • Continuously evaluates engagement
  • Predicts disengagement risk
  • Selects the next appropriate intervention
  • Adapts to individual behavior and progress

How it works

An adaptive decision layer for every patient journey.

01

Observe

Bazi processes consented behavioral, contextual, and progress signals from the therapeutic experience.

02

Predict

Models estimate when engagement is weakening before a patient fully disengages.

03

Decide

A constrained decision engine selects the next appropriate intervention from an approved therapeutic library.

04

Adapt

The product adjusts timing, sequencing, prompts, difficulty, or support based on the individual patient.

Product architecture

Built to integrate, not replace.

Bazi is an SDK and API layer for existing digital therapeutics. It adds an adaptive decision layer without requiring you to rebuild your product.

  1. Existing therapeutic application
  2. Bazi SDK
  3. Signal processing
  4. Disengagement prediction
  5. Adaptive decision engine
  6. Approved intervention library
  7. Personalized therapeutic experience
integration.js
import { Bazi } from "@bazi/sdk";

const bazi = new Bazi({
  therapyId: "rehab-program",
  mode: "on-device"
});

const decision = await bazi.recommend({
  engagementSignals,
  treatmentProgress,
  approvedInterventions
});

renderIntervention(decision.nextIntervention);
