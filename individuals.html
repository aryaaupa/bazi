WHY ADAPTIVE CARE MATTERS

A difficult week should not automatically become the end of treatment.

Digital therapeutic programs often follow a fixed sequence of sessions, reminders and exercises. But a person’s energy, schedule, symptoms and ability to participate can change during treatment.

Bazi is designed to help therapeutic software recognize patterns that may signal developing difficulty and respond using options defined in advance by the treatment provider or clinical team.

HOW ADAPTIVE TREATMENT COULD WORK

The experience changes when the pattern changes.

01

Your participation changes

You may begin completing fewer sessions, starting later, taking longer or reporting greater fatigue or difficulty.

02

The product recognizes a pattern

Instead of treating each missed or delayed session as an isolated event, the therapeutic product can consider how several signals are changing together.

03

An approved response is selected

The product may adjust pacing, change reminder timing, provide additional support or request appropriate human review.

04

The effect is evaluated

The system observes whether the approved response helps restore meaningful participation without exceeding the boundaries set by the provider.

WHAT COULD CHANGE

Adaptation does not have to mean a major treatment change.

01

Session pacing

A therapeutic product could offer a shorter or more manageable session when fatigue or difficulty increases.

02

Reminder timing

Reminder timing could be adjusted when repeated delays suggest that the current schedule is no longer effective.

03

Additional support

The experience could provide approved educational material, encouragement or recovery guidance when appropriate.

04

Human review

Certain patterns could trigger a request for review by a clinician, coach or care team rather than an automated treatment change.

PATIENT PRINCIPLES

Adaptive care should remain understandable, bounded and human-accountable.

01

Visible adaptation

People should be able to understand when and why their treatment experience has changed.

02

Defined boundaries

Available adaptations should come from options approved by the therapeutic provider or clinical team.

03

Human oversight

Significant treatment decisions should preserve appropriate human review and clear responsibility.

04

Responsible data use

Sensitive behavioral and health information should be handled with care, transparency and appropriate controls.

A person should never be reduced to an engagement score.

DATA AND PRIVACY

Adaptive technology should not require unnecessary surveillance.

Bazi is being designed to work with information already produced through a digital therapeutic experience, such as session completion, response timing, duration and user-reported difficulty.

✓
Purpose-limited signals

Data should be used for clearly defined therapeutic and product purposes rather than unrelated profiling.

✓
Appropriate controls

Therapeutic organizations should maintain access, retention and security practices appropriate to the information they process.

✓
Clear accountability

People should know which organization is responsible for the therapeutic product and how their information is being used.

CLINICAL BOUNDARIES

Bazi recommends within constraints. It does not replace a healthcare professional.

✓
It does not diagnose.

Bazi is not designed to determine whether a person has a medical condition.

✓
It does not independently prescribe.

Bazi does not create medication, therapy or treatment plans on its own.

✓
It does not invent interventions.

The system evaluates only the actions made available by the therapeutic organization or clinical team.

✓
It can support human escalation.

When configured to do so, concerning patterns can be routed for appropriate human review.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What individuals should know about Bazi.

01 ABOUT BAZI

Can I sign up for Bazi directly?

Bazi is being designed as infrastructure for digital therapeutic and healthcare organizations. It is not currently a direct-to-consumer treatment product.

02 TREATMENT CONTROL

Can Bazi change my treatment by itself?

No. The system is intended to evaluate actions defined by the therapeutic organization or clinical team and preserve appropriate human oversight.

03 CURRENT STAGE

Has Bazi established clinical efficacy?

Not yet. Bazi is at an early product-development stage and has not established clinical efficacy for its platform.

04 HEALTHCARE RELATIONSHIP

Is Bazi a healthcare provider?

No. Bazi does not provide medical care, diagnosis, prescribing or emergency services.

05 EMERGENCIES

What should I do in a medical emergency?

Bazi is not an emergency service. Contact local emergency services or an appropriate healthcare professional when urgent help is needed.
